# Memex

**Type:** historical concept / hypothetical device
**Also known as:** Memory Extender

## Description

A hypothetical personal information machine described by [[entities/vannevar-bush|Vannevar Bush]] in his 1945 essay *As We May Think*. The Memex would store all of a person's documents, books, and records on microfilm, with the ability to create "associative trails" — links between documents based on intellectual connection — and share those trails with others. It was a vision of private, curated, associatively linked knowledge — decades before hypertext or the web.

## Key Facts

- Described in *As We May Think*, published in *The Atlantic* (July 1945).
- Key innovation: associative indexing (links follow thought, not filing hierarchy).
- Never built; remained a thought experiment.
- Directly influenced Ted Nelson's hypertext, Doug Engelbart's NLS/Augment, and the World Wide Web.
- The maintenance problem (who keeps the trails current?) was never solved in Bush's vision.
