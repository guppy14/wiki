# Vannevar Bush

**Type:** person — scientist, engineer, administrator
**Also known as:** Vanevar Bush (common misspelling)

## Description

American engineer and science administrator (1890–1974), best known for directing the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during World War II and for his 1945 essay *As We May Think*, in which he described the [[entities/memex|Memex]] — a proto-hypertext personal knowledge machine.

## Key Facts

- Wrote *As We May Think* (1945), published in *The Atlantic*.
- The Memex anticipated hypertext, the web, and personal knowledge management by decades.
- His framing of associative trails — links between documents based on intellectual connection rather than filing hierarchy — is the conceptual ancestor of wiki-link graphs.
